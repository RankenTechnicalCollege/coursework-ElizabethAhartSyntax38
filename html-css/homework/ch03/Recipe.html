"THE COBB SALAD"

My favorite RECIPE is the Cobb Salad .

You will need the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup Red Wine Vinegar
  • 2 Teaspoons Salt
  • 1 teaspoon Lemon juice
  • 1 small Garlic clove minced
  • 3/4 teaspoon Ground Pepper
  • 3/4 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon Sugar
  • /4 teaspoon ground mustard
  • 3/4 cup Canola oil
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 6 1/2 cups Torn romain
  • 2 1/2 cups Torn curly endives
  • 1 bunch Watercress , 4oz, trimmed, divided
  • 2 cups cubed cooked chicken breast
  • 2 medium Tomatoes seeded and chopped
  • 1 medium Ripe alvocaldo peeled and chopped
  • 3 hard boiled large eggs, chopped
  • 1/2 cup RougeFort Cheese or crumbled blue
  • 6 bacon strips
  • 2 tablespoons Fresh Chives
DIRECTIONS ARE INCLUDE AS : STep 1 MAKE THE DRESSING
This vinaigrette couldn’t be easier to make. Combine all the ingredients (except the oils) in a blender or food processor. With the motor running, gradually add the canola and olive oils in a steady stream to create an emulsification. Store the dressing in the refrigerator until it’s time to eat. Give it a firm shake or whisk it before using it.
STEP TWO :GET CHOPPING
This is the only time-consuming part of this recipe, but it can be done in advance if you like. Store each chopped ingredient separately in airtight containers until you’re ready to compose the salad.We’ll start with the greens: Wash and chop the romaine, endive and watercress. To keep lettuce from wilting, make sure the lettuce is spun as dry as possible. Then, chop the chicken, tomatoes, avocado and eggs into 1/2- to 1-inch, bite-sized pieces. For the cheese and bacon, you won’t need a knife at all; these ingredients should crumble in your hands.
Step Three: COMPOSE AND DRESS
Now comes the fun part! Toss the lettuces together and place them in a large bowl or on a platter. Arrange each of the remaining ingredients in a pattern over the lettuce. You can make a line of each ingredient, or you can place piles of each in colorful circles. Feel free to sprinkle everything randomly, and the salad will still taste great!Finish the salad by topping it with the chives. When you’re ready to eat, drizzle 1 cup of the dressing over the salad, serving the remaining dressing on the side if you choose.
